Student-focused structure

FactsOnly the facts needed to understand the issue and holding.
IssueThe legal question the court answers.
RuleThe doctrine, test, or standard your professor may ask about.
AnalysisWhy the rule applies to the facts.
HoldingThe court's answer.
Class notesProfessor cues, questions, policy notes, and outline reminders.

How to use it before class

  1. Read the assigned case once without filling every field.
  2. Write a short issue and holding in your own words.
  3. Add rule and analysis after identifying what the professor likely cares about.
  4. Use class notes to mark what changed after discussion.
