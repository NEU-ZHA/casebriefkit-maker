What this case brief maker does

The maker gives you a simple form for the parts most law students need while reading assigned cases: facts, issue, rule, analysis, conclusion, holding, reasoning, and class notes. You can switch between FIRAC, IRAC, and CREAC depending on the class or professor.

It is built for fast study notes, not polished legal writing. The point is to reduce blank-page friction before class and keep the output easy to paste into Word, Google Docs, Notion, Markdown, or Obsidian.

Frequently asked questions

Is this better than a blank case brief template?

Use the maker when you want a filled, copy-ready output. Use the template pages when you only need a reusable outline.

What format should I copy?

Use plain text for Google Docs, Word HTML for Microsoft Word, and Markdown for Obsidian or other markdown-based notes.

Can I download a printable version?

Yes. The free PDF case brief template and DOCX case brief template are available as separate downloads.
