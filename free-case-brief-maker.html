Use it for your own notes

The maker does not read a case for you and does not promise that your answer is right. It gives structure to the notes you enter. For law school prep, that distinction matters: you still decide what the rule, holding, and reasoning are.

Free case brief maker FAQ

Is the case brief maker free?

Yes. The basic case brief maker is free to use in the browser.

Does the maker write the case brief for me?

No. It formats the notes you enter into FIRAC, IRAC, or CREAC structure. You still decide the facts, rule, holding, and reasoning.

Can I copy the output into Word or Google Docs?

Yes. The maker can produce copy-ready output for Word, Google Docs, Markdown, or plain text notes.
